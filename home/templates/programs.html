Debate

Communication is an integral part of life and a necessary skill to develop. Through debate students will learn important analysis and critical thinking skills which directly relate to their english classes. Also, debate will help students hone their skills to think on the spot and come with reasons for their opinions. Furthermore, debate will help students learn more about the world around them and will have a better understanding of how the world functions. The ability to effectively articulate your thoughts and emotions into words is key real world skill. Debate will help students gain essential skills for class presentations, interviews and everyday communication.

Type of Debate:
Tutoring the Future will focus on Lincoln-Douglas Debate. Lincoln-Douglas or LD is a one on one debate that focuses on logic, ethical issues, and philosophy. The debate topics are called resolutions and are moral statements whose values are debated. The affirmative argues that the resolution is true, arguing in favor of the resolution, while the negative argues against the resolution. LD debate concepts are applicable to other debate forms as well such as Parliamentary Debate. So if students know how to do LD debate, they will be able to easily do other debate forms. Furthermore, concepts taught in debate are universally applicable. Learning how to format arguments in a persuasive and effective way will allow students to become better writers and communicators.
